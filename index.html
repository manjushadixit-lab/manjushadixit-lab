Our lab is dedicated to unraveling the molecular underpinnings of cancer progression with a focus on key hallmarks such as angiogenesis, stemness, therapy resistance, metastasis, and genomic instability. We investigate the dynamic interplay between cytoskeletal regulators and RNA-binding proteins, with a special emphasis on the IQGAP family, FRG1, EEF1A2, and EEF1A1, which play critical roles in tumor signaling networks.

Our work spans pan-cancer analyses, with particular focus on breast cancer and the understudied but highly aggressive gallbladder cancer. We aim to delineate how these proteins modulate oncogenic signaling pathways, including the ERK, AKT, and NMD (nonsense-mediated mRNA decay) axes, to drive tumor growth and immune evasion.

By integrating multi-omics, functional genomics, and translational models, we seek to identify novel biomarkers and therapeutic vulnerabilities. Our long-term vision is to contribute to the development of precision medicine approaches tailored to difficult-to-treat cancers.
